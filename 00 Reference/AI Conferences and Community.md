# AI Conferences and Community

The AI research and practitioner community is one of the fastest-moving in science and technology. Understanding where knowledge is shared, debated, and discovered helps navigate the field effectively.

## Major Academic Conferences

### Machine Learning and AI

| Conference | Full Name | Focus | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NeurIPS** | Neural Information Processing Systems | Broad ML/AI research | Annual (December) |
| **ICML** | International Conference on Machine Learning | Core ML research | Annual (July) |
| **ICLR** | International Conference on Learning Representations | [[Deep Learning]] and representations | Annual (May) |
| **AAAI** | Association for the Advancement of AI | Broad AI | Annual (February) |

NeurIPS, ICML, and ICLR are the "big three" — papers accepted at these venues carry significant prestige. Most landmark papers ([[Attention Is All You Need]], [[Scaling Laws]], [[Chinchilla Scaling]]) were published at these conferences.

## arXiv Culture

Most AI research is published on **arXiv** (arxiv.org) before (or instead of) conference submission. This creates an unusual dynamic:

- Papers are public within hours of completion
- Peer review happens informally on social media
- The "publish first, review later" model means the pace is extreme
- Reading arXiv daily is standard for active researchers

Key sections:
- `cs.CL` — Computation and Language ([[Natural Language Processing|NLP]], [[Large Language Models|LLMs]])
- `cs.LG` — Machine Learning
- `cs.CV` — [[Computer Vision]]
- `cs.AI` — Artificial Intelligence
- `cs.CR` — Cryptography and Security (for [[AI and Security]])
